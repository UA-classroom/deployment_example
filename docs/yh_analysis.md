# Swedish Yrkeshogskola (YH) - System Analysis

Research compiled for improving `plan.md` to build a management system for a typical Swedish vocational school (yrkeshogskola).

---

## 1. What is a Yrkeshogskola?

Higher Vocational Education (yrkeshogskolan) is a post-secondary education form that combines theoretical studies with workplace-based learning (LIA). It is governed by MYH (Myndigheten for yrkeshogskolan - the Swedish National Agency for Higher Vocational Education) under Regulation (2009:130).

Key characteristics:
- Programs are designed in close cooperation with employers/industry
- Strong emphasis on labor market relevance
- Publicly funded and free for students
- Students are eligible for CSN student financial aid
- Education providers can be municipalities, universities, or private companies

Sources: [MYH English overview](https://www.myh.se/in-english/higher-vocational-education-yrkeshogskolan), [Yrkeshogskolan.se](https://www.yrkeshogskolan.se/in-english)

## 2. Program Structure

### Duration and Credits (YH-poang)
- 5 YH-credits = 1 week of full-time study
- 100 credits = 6 months full-time
- 200 credits = 1 year full-time
- 400 credits = 2 years full-time
- Most programs are 1-3 years

### Program Types
| Type | Credits | Diploma | SeQF Level |
|------|---------|---------|------------|
| Short course (kurs) | Up to 99 | No diploma (utbildningsbevis only) | - |
| Standard program | 200+ (min 1 year) | Yrkeshogskoleexamen | 5 |
| Qualified program | 400+ (min 2 years) | Kvalificerad yrkeshogskoleexamen | 6 |

### Diploma Requirements
- **Yrkeshogskoleexamen** (200+ credits): Must include independent thesis work. All courses passed.
- **Kvalificerad yrkeshogskoleexamen** (400+ credits): Must include independent thesis, minimum 25% LIA (100+ credits), all courses passed.
- **Utbildningsbevis** (education certificate): Issued when student completes some but not all courses, or for shorter programs under 200 credits.

The ledningsgrupp (steering group) issues both diplomas and certificates.

Source: [MYH - Examen](https://www.myh.se/yrkeshogskolan/for-utbildningsanordare/examen)

## 3. Utbildningsplan (Education Plan) - Required Content

Every program must have a comprehensive education plan specifying:
- Responsible education provider (and any co-providers)
- Program objectives and focus areas
- Included courses (list)
- Total scope (credits), structure, and organization
- Hours of instructor-led or mentor-guided instruction
- Prerequisite/eligibility requirements
- Selection criteria for applicants
- Specific exam requirements (if applicable)
- Quality assurance mechanisms

The education plan is established once the application to MYH is approved and is not updated afterward.

Source: [MYH - Utbildningsplan, kursplaner och betyg](https://www.myh.se/yrkeshogskolan/for-utbildningsanordare/utbildningsplan-kursplaner-och-betyg)

## 4. Kursplan (Course Plan/Syllabus) - Required Content

Each course within a program needs its own course plan containing:
- Course name
- YH-credits (poang)
- Course objectives (kursmaal)
- Primary content description
- Language of instruction (if not Swedish)
- Grading principles and assessment methods

Source: [MYH - Utbildningsplan, kursplaner och betyg](https://www.myh.se/yrkeshogskolan/for-utbildningsanordare/utbildningsplan-kursplaner-och-betyg)

## 5. Grading System (Betygsskala)

### Scale
| Grade | Swedish | English |
|-------|---------|---------|
| IG | Icke godkant | Not approved / Fail |
| G | Godkant | Approved / Pass |
| VG | Val godkant | Well approved / Pass with distinction |

### Rules
- Grades can only be set when: (1) the course is completed AND (2) complete documentation exists for assessment
- If at least one course objective is not met, the grade must be IG
- Some courses may only use IG/G (no VG) if the course content does not support distinguishing VG
- Providers have 4 weeks to report grades after they are assigned
- Students should receive feedback within 4 weeks after course conclusion
- Grade increases can be done by the educator directly
- Grade reductions require a formal change request to MYH with supporting documentation

Source: [MYH - Betygssattning YH](https://fragor.myh.se/guide/ua-admin-betygssattning-yh)

## 6. LIA (Larande i Arbete - Workplace Learning)

### What It Is
LIA is integrated workplace-based learning - not a separate internship but embedded curriculum where theory meets practice. Students develop knowledge, skills, and competencies in a real work environment.

### Requirements
- **Qualified programs** (400+ credits): Minimum 100 credits (25%) must be LIA
- **Standard programs**: LIA is common but not always mandatory
- **Short courses** (under 99 credits): LIA is optional

### Responsibilities

**Education provider (utbildningsanordnare):**
- Develop formal course plans for LIA with objectives
- Design grading criteria
- Ensure quality of LIA placements
- Actively participate in planning and organizing LIA
- Ultimately responsible for securing LIA placements for students

**Employer/workplace:**
- Provide real working conditions
- Designate a LIA supervisor (handledare) to guide students
- Participate actively in LIA planning and execution

### Grading
LIA is graded like any other course (IG/G/VG) based on whether course objectives are met. The education provider sets the grade based on workplace assessment and documentation.

Source: [MYH - LIA](https://www.myh.se/yrkeshogskolan/for-utbildningsanordare/larande-i-arbete-lia)

## 7. Organizational Roles

### Ledningsgrupp (Steering Group / Board)
**Composition requirements:**
- Majority must be representatives from relevant labor market/employers
- At least one student representative per program
- Representatives from the education provider
- The utbildningsledare (education leader)

**Responsibilities:**
- Overall responsibility for ensuring education objectives are met
- Admit applicants to the program (final admission decisions)
- Issue examination certificates and education certificates
- Systematic quality assurance work
- Ensure graduate competencies align with labor market demands
- Plan, organize, and supervise the education

Governed by Chapter 4 of Regulation (2009:130).

Source: [MYH - Ledningsgruppsarbete](https://www.myh.se/yrkeshogskolan/for-utbildningsanordare/ledningsgruppsarbete)

### Utbildningsledare (Education Leader)
- Appointed by the education provider
- Leads the daily operational work of the program
- Works for continuous development of the education
- Must be a member of the ledningsgrupp
- De facto program manager: coordinates teachers, schedules, student issues

### Other Common Roles (not formally regulated but typical)
- **Kursansvarig** (Course responsible): Teacher responsible for a specific course
- **LIA-samordnare** (LIA coordinator): Manages LIA placements, employer contacts
- **Larare/Forelasare** (Teacher/Lecturer): Often industry professionals, not full-time academics
- **Studeranderepresentant** (Student representative): Elected student on the ledningsgrupp
- **Utbildningsanordnare** (Education provider): The organization running the program

## 8. Admission Process (Antagning)

### Eligibility (Behorighet)
**Basic eligibility (grundlaggande behorighet):**
- Completed upper secondary education (gymnasieexamen) or equivalent
- Broad pathways in: vocational gymnasium graduates especially welcomed
- Alternative routes possible (work experience, foreign credentials, etc.)

**Special eligibility (sarskild behorighet):**
- Additional requirements set by the education provider for specific programs
- E.g., prior knowledge in specific subjects, work experience

### Selection Process (Urval)
When more eligible applicants than seats:
1. Provider applies selection criteria (varies by program)
2. May include: grades, written tests, interviews, prior education, work experience
3. Ledningsgrupp makes final admission decisions
4. Admission notifications sent via email

Source: [MYH - Tilltrade](https://www.myh.se/yrkeshogskolan/for-utbildningsanordare/tilltrade-till-utbildning)

## 9. CSN Reporting Requirements

Education providers must report to CSN (student financial aid agency):
- Student enrollment status
- Study time and scope (full-time/part-time)
- Study activity and attendance
- Study results (credits completed)
- Interruptions or discontinuations (avbrott)
- School and program information

Reporting is done via "Mina tjanster" portal. Timely reporting is critical as it directly affects students' financial aid eligibility. Unjustified absence can lead to withdrawal of student aid.

Source: [CSN - Rapportering](https://www.csn.se/partner/rapporterar-uppgifter-till-csn/yrkeshogskola-och-ovriga-eftergymnasiala-skolor.html)

## 10. Ledningsgrupp - Detailed Composition Rules

### Mandatory Members (one role per person - no doubling)

1. **Utbildningsledare** (Education Leader) - leads daily operations, drives program development
2. **Student representative(s)** - at least one per program, full voting equality with other members
3. **School system representative** - at least one (required for programs leading to yrkeshogskoleexamen)
   - Must have knowledge of upper secondary and adult education
   - Must have subject matter expertise in the program's field
   - Purpose: ensure YH education maintains a higher/broader level than gymnasium
4. **Higher education representative** - at least one from university/college (required ONLY for kvalificerad yrkeshogskoleexamen programs)
   - Ensures education is grounded in scientific knowledge
   - Defines boundaries between vocational and higher education
   - Oversees credit recognition from university studies
5. **Employer representatives** - multiple, must constitute a **majority** of all members (non-negotiable)

### Exceptions
- Exemptions only for narrow occupational fields (e.g., goldsmithing, millinery) preserving specialized craft knowledge
- Exemptions only apply to school system or higher education representatives
- Employer majority participation is ALWAYS mandatory

Source: [MYH - Ledningsgruppens sammansattning](https://www.myh.se/yrkeshogskolan/for-utbildningsanordare/anordnarstod/stodartiklar/ledningsgruppens-sammansattning-krav-samt-mojligheter-till-undantag)

## 11. Study Breaks and Interruptions

### Study Break (Studieuppehall)
- Temporary pause in studies
- Student retains connection to the program
- Documented agreement between student and provider
- Provider must discuss curriculum changes if student returns to a different cohort
- Agreement must specify which curriculum version governs the student's final examination
- Students returning after break are registered with a new start date in the current cohort
- Previously completed courses are credited toward their qualification
- **MYH grants no additional study places for returning students** - they must fit within existing capacity

### Study Interruption (Studieavbrott)
- Definitive break from studies
- Student forfeits their place in the program
- Must be reported to CSN and MYH
- Provider cannot claim state subsidies for students who have discontinued

### Documentation Requirements
- All agreements with students must be documented
- Providers must maintain documented procedures for handling breaks, interruptions, and re-enrollment
- Routines must clarify what constitutes active participation
- Information about interruptions/breaks must be updated continuously in administrative system
- Changes reported to CSN as they directly affect student financial aid

Source: [MYH - Studieuppehall och studieavbrott](https://www.myh.se/yrkeshogskolan/for-utbildningsanordare/studieuppehall-och-studieavbrott)

## 12. Validation and Credit Recognition (Tillgodoraknande)

### What It Is
Validation is the process of identifying, assessing, and formally recognizing an individual's actual knowledge, skills, and abilities regardless of how they were acquired:
- **Formal learning**: organized education within public system
- **Non-formal learning**: organized education outside public system
- **Informal learning**: from work or everyday life

### Credit Recognition Process
1. Student provides documentation (work certificates, course completion docs, volunteer service letters, work samples)
2. Provider evaluates competence against course objectives and grading criteria
3. If documentation is insufficient, provider may conduct competence mapping dialogues and additional assessments
4. Credited competence is graded using standard IG/G/VG scale
5. Becomes part of the student's formal educational record

### Scope
Credit recognition may apply to:
- Parts of individual courses
- Complete courses
- Multiple courses

### YH-flex (Accelerated Pathway)
For students with extensive professional experience (~50% of program content):
1. Competence mapping (tests, oral interviews)
2. Competence assessment against course learning outcomes
3. Determination of which courses need completing vs can be credited
4. Shortened individual study plan

Source: [MYH - Reell kompetens och tillgodoraknande](https://www.myh.se/yrkeshogskolan/for-utbildningsanordare/validering-och-reell-kompetens/reell-kompetens-och-tillgodoraknande)

## 13. Special Pedagogical Support (SPS)

Students with documented disabilities (lasting 6+ months) have the legal right to special pedagogical support. The purpose is to place students in a comparable situation with others regarding knowledge acquisition and examination.

- **Student responsibility**: must inform the school of difficulties and need for support, must provide documentation
- **Provider responsibility**: ensure accessible premises and accessible digital teaching materials
- **Funding**: providers can apply for reimbursement from MYH for SPS costs

Source: [MYH - Sarskilt pedagogiskt stod](https://www.myh.se/yrkeshogskolan/for-utbildningsanordare/sarskilt-pedagogiskt-stod-sps)

---

## 14. Student Insurance

Students in YH programs are covered by insurance:
- **Personal injury insurance**: covers accidents during school time and direct travel between home and school/LIA workplace. No deductible.
- **Liability insurance during LIA**: covers damage caused by the student at the workplace during LIA. Only applies in Sweden, non-governmental workplaces.
- **Student UT insurance**: for studies or LIA abroad, covers personal injury + travel insurance.

Source: [MYH - Forsakringar](https://www.myh.se/yrkeshogskolan/for-utbildningsanordare/forsakringar-for-studerande)

## 15. Quality Assurance System

### MYH Inspections
MYH conducts quality reviews based on established quality criteria examining how providers implement systematic quality work.

### Self-Evaluation
- Voluntary Excel-based diagnostic tool (version 3.0, January 2025)
- Organized around themes with questions based on MYH quality criteria
- Includes tracking student throughput data
- Links to key documentation

### Key Quality Areas
- Education outcomes and employment success
- Student influence (studerandeinflytande)
- Course evaluations (kursutvarderingar)
- Accessibility
- Gender equality in career choices
- LIA quality

### Student Influence (Studerandeinflytande)
- Regulated by law
- Students must have opportunity for insight into operations
- Students must be able to influence educational content and implementation
- Course and term evaluations are mandatory tools for continuous improvement

Source: [MYH - Kvalitetsframjande stod](https://www.myh.se/yrkeshogskolan/for-utbildningsanordare/kvalitetsframjande-stod-och-verktyg)

## 16. Cohort Management and MYH Reporting

### Decision Years and Cohorts (Omgangar)
- Programs are organized into decision years (when MYH approved the program)
- Each decision year can have multiple cohorts
- All cohorts must conclude within the decision's validity period
- Programs (100+ credits): cohorts assigned by MYH
- Courses (under 99 credits): providers create their own cohorts

### Required Administrative Roles on MYH Mina Sidor
- "Hantera beviljad utbildning YH" (Manage approved YH education)
- "Hantera studiedokumentation YH" (Manage YH student documentation)

### Reporting Timeline

**Before cohort starts:**
- Register ledningsgrupp members (may note "vacant" for student rep)
- Register contact persons and education leader
- Add marketing information (description, logo, website)
- Set study format, pace, start/end dates

**At cohort start (start signal):**
- Report applications received (by gender)
- Report enrolled students (by gender)
- Report actual start date, preliminary end date
- Ledningsgrupp must be registered before start signal
- Student data must be established within 4 weeks of start

**During cohort:**
- Report grades as soon as possible after course completion (within 4 weeks)
- Update student records continuously (absences, deferrals, new enrollees)
- Submit subsidy requisition (earliest 3 weeks after start, then every 6 months)

**At cohort end (end signal):**
- Finalize all grades and completion data
- Report examination/diploma issuance decision date
- Conduct 6-month follow-up on graduate employment

### Student Registration Process on Mina Sidor
1. Enter personal numbers (12-digit personnummer or samordningsnummer)
2. System retrieves data from the Tax Agency (Skatteverket)
3. Review retrieved data, add deviating start dates or study locations
4. Save student list (can still remove students)
5. Confirm and finalize (no removal possible after confirmation)

Students with protected personal information appear with a protection symbol.

Source: [MYH Handbok](https://minasidor.myh.se/hjalp-och-information/guider/handbok-yh/)

## 17. Diploma Certificates - Detailed Requirements

### Europass Supplement
Since January 2024, all diploma recipients must receive Europass qualification supplements in both Swedish and English. Providers create, revise, and maintain these documents using MYH-provided templates aligned with SeQF levels 5-6.

### Certificate Content (regulated by)
- Regulation (2009:130) on Swedish Vocational Education (YHF)
- MYHFS 2023:4 (Authority's regulations on reporting student information and study results)

### Issuance
- MYH provides a digital output function for printing certificates based on reported student data
- Word templates available in Swedish and English
- Standardized design for employer recognition
- Ledningsgrupp formally issues both diploma and education certificates

Source: [MYH - Examensbevis och utbildningsbevis](https://www.myh.se/yrkeshogskolan/for-utbildningsanordare/examen/examensbevis-och-utbildningsbevis)

## 18. LIA - Additional Details on Responsibility

### Ultimate Provider Responsibility
- The education provider bears **final responsibility** for ensuring students have LIA placements when courses begin
- Even if students are encouraged to find placements independently, the provider is the last resort
- Provider must treat LIA like any other course: ensuring students receive instruction and achieve learning objectives

### When Students Refuse Offered Placements
- If the provider offers a quality-assured placement and the student refuses, the provider is considered to have fulfilled regulatory requirements

### Alternative Arrangements
- When a student cannot complete standard LIA, providers must explore alternative course options
- Requires a written mutual agreement (individual study plan)
- Plan must include complete course planning through graduation
- Must account for any prerequisite requirements

Source: [MYH - Ansvar for LIA](https://fragor.myh.se/guide/studerande-yh-slutligt-ansvar-lia)

## 20. Typical Student Journey (Timeline)

```
1. DISCOVERY (anytime)
   - Student finds program via yrkeshogskolan.se, provider website, or studera.nu
   - Reviews program info, eligibility requirements, application deadlines

2. APPLICATION (typically Feb-May)
   - Student applies directly to the education provider
   - Submits required documentation (grades, work experience, etc.)
   - Application deadline usually April or May

3. ELIGIBILITY CHECK
   - Provider assesses basic eligibility (gymnasieexamen or equivalent)
   - Provider checks special eligibility if applicable
   - Decision: eligible or ineligible

4. SELECTION (if oversubscribed)
   - Provider applies selection criteria (grades, tests, interviews, experience)
   - Ledningsgrupp makes final admission decision
   - Results: admitted / waitlisted / rejected

5. ADMISSION NOTIFICATION
   - Student receives admission decision via email
   - Must confirm acceptance within deadline
   - Applies for CSN student financial aid

6. PROGRAM START (typically Aug-Sep)
   - Student registers / enrolls
   - Provider reports start signal to MYH
   - Provider reports enrollment to CSN
   - Student data established in MYH Mina Sidor within 4 weeks

7. STUDIES (1-3 years)
   - Alternating theory courses and LIA periods
   - Continuous attendance tracking
   - Course evaluations after each course
   - Grades reported within 4 weeks of course completion
   - CSN reporting ongoing (study activity, results)
   - Mid-program: often LIA 1 (first workplace period)
   - Late program: LIA 2 (second workplace period, often leads to employment)
   - Thesis/independent work (examensarbete)

8. GRADUATION
   - All courses passed? -> Eligible for examensbevis (diploma)
   - Some courses incomplete? -> Eligible for utbildningsbevis (certificate)
   - Ledningsgrupp formally issues diploma/certificate
   - Europass supplement issued (Swedish + English)
   - Provider reports end signal to MYH
   - 6-month follow-up on employment status

ALTERNATIVE PATHS:
   - Study break (studieuppehall) -> returns to later cohort
   - Study interruption (studieavbrott) -> leaves program, loses place
   - Credit recognition (tillgodoraknande) -> shortened study plan
   - YH-flex -> accelerated path for experienced professionals
   - Re-examination -> retake failed courses
```

## 21. Digital Systems in Use

YH providers typically use a combination of:
- **MYH Mina Sidor**: mandatory reporting platform for student data, grades, subsidies, cohort management
- **CSN Mina Tjanster**: mandatory reporting platform for student financial aid data
- **LMS** (Learning Management System): Canvas, Vklass, or similar for course content and assignments
- **Administrative platform**: SchoolSoft, custom system, or similar for attendance, schedules, internal student records
- The management system we are building would replace/supplement the internal administrative platform

## 22. Gap Analysis: Current plan.md vs Real YH System

### What plan.md does well
- Role system with admin, utbildningsledare, student (correct core roles)
- Programs with YH-credits, duration, status lifecycle
- Courses with YH-credits and sort ordering within programs
- Leader assignment to programs (utbildningsledare)
- Proper role-based access control

### What the plan.md is missing or could improve

#### High Priority (core YH functionality)

1. **LIA model and management**
   - LIA courses need special handling: workplace, company, supervisor (handledare), period dates
   - LIA placement tracking: which student is placed where, with whom
   - LIA-specific grading workflow (provider grades, but workplace provides assessment input)
   - The plan mentions "Later phases will add LIA" but the Course model has no `is_lia` flag or distinction

2. **Grading system**
   - No grade model exists yet (mentioned as future phase)
   - Should support IG/G/VG scale
   - Grades are per-student per-course
   - Need grade history / audit trail (grade changes require documentation)
   - Re-examination support

3. **Student enrollment / program membership**
   - No student-to-program association model
   - Students need to be enrolled in a specific program (and by extension, its courses)
   - A "class" or "cohort" concept (omgang/klass) is typical: e.g., "SUVNET24" means the 2024 cohort of a program
   - Enrollment status: active, on leave, graduated, dropped out

4. **Admission (antagning)**
   - Application model: applicants apply to programs
   - Eligibility assessment workflow
   - Selection/ranking when oversubscribed
   - Admission decision by ledningsgrupp
   - Status flow: applied -> eligible/ineligible -> selected/waitlisted -> admitted/rejected -> enrolled

5. **Attendance / study activity tracking**
   - Required for CSN reporting
   - Providers must track and report student activity
   - Absence tracking with justified/unjustified distinction

#### Medium Priority (important for completeness)

6. **Ledningsgrupp representation**
   - The current plan has no concept of the steering group
   - In practice, the system may not need to model all members, but awareness of this governance layer matters
   - Admission decisions and diploma issuance are formally ledningsgrupp responsibilities

7. **Course plan details**
   - Current Course model is lightweight (name, code, description, points, sort_order)
   - Missing: course objectives (kursmaal), assessment methods, language of instruction
   - These could be optional text fields or a separate "syllabus" section

8. **Diploma / certificate issuance**
   - Logic to determine if a student qualifies for yrkeshogskoleexamen vs utbildningsbevis
   - Check: all courses passed? Total credits met? Thesis completed? LIA percentage met?
   - Certificate generation

9. **CSN reporting data**
   - Study scope (full-time percentage)
   - Enrollment confirmations
   - Interruption reporting
   - Results reporting

10. **Program status lifecycle**
    - Current: draft/active/completed/archived (good)
    - Could add: "approved" (by MYH) as a status between draft and active
    - Each program round/cohort may have its own lifecycle

#### Lower Priority (nice to have)

11. **Independent thesis (examensarbete/sjalvstandigt arbete)**
    - Required for both diploma types
    - Could be modeled as a special course type

12. **Schedule / calendar**
    - Course periods, LIA periods, examination dates
    - Not strictly needed in phase 1 but very useful operationally

13. **Employer / company registry**
    - For LIA placements
    - Company name, contact person, address
    - History of which students were placed there

14. **Teacher/instructor management**
    - Many YH teachers are industry professionals (not full-time staff)
    - Course-to-teacher assignment
    - Could be as simple as a user with a specific role assigned to courses

## 25. Summary

The current `plan.md` provides a solid Phase 1 foundation (roles, programs, courses). However, a complete YH management system needs significantly more domain modeling. The following areas should be considered for future phases:

### Phase 2 Candidates (core student management)
1. **Student enrollment & cohort management** - students belong to cohorts within programs, with status tracking (active/on leave/graduated/dropped out)
2. **Grading system** - IG/G/VG per student per course, with audit trail, re-examination support, 4-week reporting deadline
3. **Attendance tracking** - required for CSN compliance, justified/unjustified absence distinction

### Phase 3 Candidates (LIA and admissions)
4. **LIA management** - placement tracking, company registry, supervisor (handledare) contacts, LIA-specific course handling, provider ultimate responsibility
5. **Admission workflow** - application, eligibility check, selection, ledningsgrupp decision, enrollment

### Phase 4 Candidates (compliance and advanced features)
6. **Study breaks & interruptions** - studieuppehall/studieavbrott with documentation, individual study plans, CSN notification
7. **Credit recognition (tillgodoraknande)** - validation of prior learning, shortened study plans, YH-flex support
8. **Diploma/certificate issuance** - automated eligibility checking, Europass supplements, ledningsgrupp approval
9. **CSN reporting data** - enrollment confirmations, study scope, interruptions, results
10. **Course evaluations** - mandatory student feedback collection, quality improvement tracking

### Out of Scope (but good to know about)
11. **Ledningsgrupp membership tracking** - formal composition rules, meeting management
12. **SPS (Special pedagogical support)** - disability accommodations, MYH reimbursement
13. **Insurance tracking** - student insurance during LIA
14. **International exchange programs** - Europass, student mobility
15. **Financial management** - MYH subsidies, requisitions, studerandeavgifter

### Key Insight
The system we are building serves the **utbildningsanordnare** (education provider) perspective. It should help them:
- Manage programs, courses, and cohorts
- Track students through their full lifecycle
- Handle LIA placements and employer relationships
- Maintain grading records with proper audit trails
- Generate data needed for MYH and CSN reporting
- Support quality assurance through course evaluations and student influence

The plan already acknowledges that LIA and grades are "later phases," which is the right phasing approach. This analysis provides the domain knowledge needed to design those phases correctly when the time comes.
